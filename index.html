Portfolio

These are a couple of my projects that I have had a lot of fun working on over the years. Some range from classwork, to just for fun.

Chess

A Chess bot using Game theory and Parallelism
to beat other bots.

PiGRRL 2

A handheld gaming emulator using
custom made parts, can hold over 3000 games.

Polynomial Calculator

A Polynomial Calculator written in Ruby.
Can add, subtract, multiply and do much more!

Dynamic Storage Allocator

Implemented a memory allocator using an explicit free list. While improving my C programming skills, including gaining more experience with structs, pointers, macros, and debugging.

Twitter Retweet Bot

Created a twitter bot, that when given a keyword.
Would retweet, follow, and like the tweet.
I used this to enter giveaways,
and to find photos of puppies!

Naive Bayes Classifiers

Used the Naive Bayes Classifier to
implement a spam filter
that learns word spam probabilities
from a prelabeled training data
and then predicts the label (ham or spam)
of a set of emails that it hasn’t seen before.

About Me

Born and raised in Seattle, WA. I was first introduced to Computer Science during my senior year of High School.
I had an exploring computer science class that Microsoft TEALS volunteers ran. I graduated from high school knowing I wanted to study computer science. I interned at Microsoft the summer after graduation and enrolled at The University of Washington apart from the 3rd cohort of the U.W Stars program. From there I was accepted into the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science and Engineering after my first year. Since then I have interned at Microsoft in the WDG org and have taught classes at my University.
Currently, I am a Software Engineer Intern at T-Mobile working in their technology department developing automation testing on new mobile devices. After graduation, I would like to go into industry and pursue my masters after a couple of years.
